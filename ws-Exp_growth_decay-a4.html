    Worksheet 5.2 Exponential growth and decay - Practice exercises
    A4US

    1.

    A signal is sent down a fiber optic cable. Its strength decreases by 2% each mile it travels. (Say it was one unit strong to start.) Story also appears in A.8.3.b

    (a)

    Name the variables, including units.

    (b)

    Make a table showing the strength of the signal over the first five miles.

    (c)

    Write an equation relating the variables.

    (d)

    The signal will need a booster (something to make the signal stronger again) when it has fallen to under 0.75 units. How far along the cable should the booster be placed? Set up and solve an equation.

    (e)

    The problem continues ...
    What is the half-life (or should we say half-distance) of a signal? That means, how far can it travel without dropping below 50%? (That will not actually happen because we would boost the signal.) Again, set up and solve an equation.

    (f)

    Draw a graph illustrating the relationship.

    (g)

    Indicate the points on your graph where you can check your answers to parts (c) and (d).

    2.

    A recent news report stated that cell phone usage is growing exponentially in developing countries. In one small country, 50,000 people owned a cell phone in the year 2000. It was estimated that usage would increase at 1.4% percent per year.

    (a)

    Name the variables including units.

    (b)

    Assuming the growth is exponential, write an equation for the function.

    (c)

    At this rate, how many years would it take for the number of people owning a cell phone to double? That is called the doubling time. Show how to set up and solve an equation to find the answer.

    (d)

    In 2011, about 682,000 people owned a cellphone. Is that count higher or lower than predicted from your equation? Explain.

    (e)

    Based on the 2011 data, would you say that cell phone usage was growing slower or faster than 1.4%?

Assume that this statement means the decrease is exponential and that the survival rate is 100% if the defibrillator is used immediately.
    Source: American Red Cross

    (a)

    Name the variables, including units, and write an equation.

    (b)

    If it takes the paramedics 2 minutes to use the defibrillator, what is the personâs chance of survival?

    (c)

    Use successive approximation to estimate to the nearest minute when the survival rate drops below 50%. Display your guesses in a table.

    (d)

    Solve your equation to find exactly when the survival rate drops below 50%.
