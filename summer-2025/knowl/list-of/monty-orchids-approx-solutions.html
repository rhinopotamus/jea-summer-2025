Worksheet Exercise 3.

Monty hopes to grow orchids but they are fragile plants. He will consider his greenhouse a success if at least nine of the ten orchids survive. Assuming the orchids each survive at rate \(S\text{,}\) the probability his greenhouse is a success, \(P\text{,}\) is given by
\begin{equation*} P= 10S^9-9S^{10} \end{equation*}
(Story also appears in 2.3.3)

(a)

Monty can buy orchids each with survival rate of \(S=0.8\text{.}\) Is that enough to give probability \(P\ge 0.8\) of a successful greenhouse?

(b)

What quality of orchids would Monty need to have probability \(P\ge 0.8\) of a successful greenhouse? Report your answer accurate to two decimal places.

(c)

What quality of orchids would Monty need to have probability \(P\ge 0.95\) of a successful greenhouse? Report your answer accurate to three decimal places.
