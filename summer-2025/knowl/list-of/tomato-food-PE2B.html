Worksheet Exercise 2.

Xander grows tomatoes in his garden. He’s noticed that a typical plant yields 5 pounds of tomatoes. He’s been experimenting with the impact of liquid food on plant yield and estimates that each drop increases yield by 14%.

(a)

Name the variables, including units and dependence.

(b)

Calculate the growth factor and write an equation showing how yield for each tomato plant depends on the number of drops of liquid food.

(c)

Xander uses 10 drops of food on one of his tomato plans and uses all of the tomatoes from that plant to make salsa. If each pound of tomatoes makes around a pint of salsa, how much salsa will Xander have (from that one plant)?

(d)

Convert your answer into gallons. Use 1 gallon = 4 quarts and 1 quart = 2 pints.

(e)

The problem continues ...
Make a table showing Xander’s projections for yield for each tomato plant if he uses 0, 1, 2, 5, or 10 drops of liquid food.
