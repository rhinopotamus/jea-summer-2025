Worksheet Exercise 3.

Our investment club has been tracking the performance of a biofuel company’s stock over the past year. Using an econometrics software package, we found the equation
\begin{equation*} V =0.00004T^3 + 0.01T^2 - 0.9T + 31 \end{equation*}
which describes the value of each share of stock $\(V\) as a function of time \(T\text{,}\) measured in weeks starting exactly one year ago.

(a)

Name the variables \(V\) and \(T\text{,}\) including units and dependence.

(b)

Complete the following table of values.
\(T\) 0 13 26 39 52
\(V\) 31.00 21.08 \(\fillinmath{\begin{array}{c}xxxx\\xxxx\end{array}}\) \(\fillinmath{\begin{array}{c}xxxx\\xxxx\end{array}}\) 16.86

(c)

Draw a graph showing how the value changed during the past year.

(d)

The problem continues ...
According to the table, what was the value of the stock when we began tracking it? What is it worth now?

(e)

We are thinking about buying some stock now, and selling it in 10 weeks. Does the equation say that’s a good idea? Explain.
Hint.
10 weeks from now is not \(T=10\) because we started counting weeks one year ago.

(f)

Looking back over the past year, how low did the value of the stock get? Use successive approximation to estimate to the nearest cent.
