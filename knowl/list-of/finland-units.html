Worksheet Exercise 4.

Jenna is studying in Finland this term and rented an older car to drive.

(a)

She learns that no matter what the road signs might say, the maximum speed limit in Finland in winter is never more than 100 km/hr. How fast is that in miles per hour (mph)? Use 1 mile \(\approx\) 1.609 km.

(b)

Jenna’s car holds 62 liters of gasoline in its tank. How many gallons is that? Use 1 liter \(\approx\) 1.057 quarts and 1 gallon = 4 quarts.

(c)

Her car gets 7.6 km/liter. Convert to miles per gallon (mpg).

(d)

Gas prices in Finland were 1.658 €/liter. What’s the equivalent price in $/gal? The symbol € stands for euro. Use 1 € \(\approx\) $1.23.

(e)

What would it cost Jenna, in euros, for a full tank of gas? How much is that in dollars?
