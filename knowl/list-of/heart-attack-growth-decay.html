Worksheet Exercise 3.

If a person has a heart attack and their heart stops beating, the amount of time it takes paramedics to restart their heart with a defibrillator is critical. Each minute that passes decreases the person’s chance of survival by 10%. Assume that this statement means the decrease is exponential and that the survival rate is 100% if the defibrillator is used immediately.

(a)

Name the variables, including units, and write an equation.

(b)

If it takes the paramedics 2 minutes to use the defibrillator, what is the person’s chance of survival?

(c)

Use successive approximation to estimate to the nearest minute when the survival rate drops below 50%. Display your guesses in a table.

(d)

Solve your equation to find exactly when the survival rate drops below 50%.
