Worksheet Exercise 4.

You and two buddies each invite 10 people to “like” your online group. Suppose everyone accepts and then they each invite 10 people. And then everyone accepts and they each invite 10 people. And so on. Of course, there is likely to be substantial overlap, but for the moment pretend that there is not any overlap.

(a)

There are 3 friends to start. In the first round they each invite 10 friends, so a total of 30 new people “like” your online group in the first round. How many new people “like” your group in the second round? The third?

(b)

Name the variables and write an equation showing how the number of new people increases in each round. Think of the original 3 friends as round 0.

(c)

Make a table showing this information. Continue your table to include the number of new people who “like” your group in the fourth and fifth rounds.

(d)

What is the total number of people who “like” your online group after five rounds? Hint: add

(e)

Comment on why our assumption is unrealistic.
