Worksheet Exercise 3.

In a shot put competition, athletes try to throw a heavy metal ball as far as possible. For one athlete the ball closely followed the parabolic arch given by the equation
\begin{equation*} H = -0.015D^{2}+1.04D+5.9 \end{equation*}
where \(D\) was the distance the ball had travelled horizontally and \(H\) was the height of the ball above ground, both in feet. The path of the ball is graphed below.

(a)

How far away did the ball land? Estimate the answer from the graph. Then show how to set up and solve an equation to find the answer to two decimal places.

(b)

How high up did the ball get? Show how to find the exact answer. Then compare to the graph.
