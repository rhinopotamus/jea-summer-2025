Worksheet Exercise 4.

After China, India, and the United States, the next five most populous countries (in 2011) are Indonesia, Brazil, Pakistan, Nigeria, and Bangladesh. Their projected growth rates and corresponding equation are listed below. Here \(Q\) is the population measured in millions and \(T\) is time measured in years since 2011.
\(4^{\text{th}}\) Indonesia   pop. 248 million   growth rate 1.04%   \(Q= 248 \ast 1.0104^T\)
\(5^{\text{th}}\) Brazil pop. 205 million growth rate 1.10% \(Q = 205 \ast 1.0110^T\)
\(6^{\text{th}}\) Pakistan pop. 190 million growth rate 1.55% \(Q = 190 \ast 1.0155^T\)
\(7^{\text{th}}\) Nigeria pop. 170 million growth rate 2.55% \(Q = 170 \ast 1.0255^T\)
\(8^{\text{th}}\) Bangladesh pop. 161 million growth rate 1.58% \(Q = 161\ast 1.0158^T\)

(a)

Which of these countries is projected to have the largest population in 2020? In 2030? In 2050?

(b)

Explain why Bangladesh’s population will not overtake Nigeria’s, assuming these projections are accurate.

(c)

Approximately when will Brazil’s population top 500 million? Will Nigeria get there first? Display your work in a successive approximation table.
