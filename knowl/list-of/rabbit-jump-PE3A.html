Worksheet Exercise 4.

A rabbit jumps so that her height above ground is given by the formula
\begin{equation*} R = 17.6T - 22T^2 \end{equation*}
where \(R=\) height of rabbit (feet above ground) and \(T=\) time (seconds).

(a)

At what height did the rabbit start her jump?

(b)

Can the rabbit jump over a 3 foot fence? Calculate the exact maximum height of the rabbit to decide.

(c)

How long does it take the rabbit to get 2 feet in the air, and when is she at that height again? Set up and solve the appropriate equation to find the answers.
