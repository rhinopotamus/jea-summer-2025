Exercise 9.

After his first beer, Stephen’s blood alcohol content (BAC) was already 0.04 and as he continued to drink, his BAC level rose 45% per hour. The equation is
\begin{equation*} S = 0.04 \ast 1.45^H \end{equation*}
where \(S\) is Stephen’s BAC and \(H\) is the time, measured in hours.
Story also appears in 1.1 #4 and 3.4 #1
(a)
Make a table showing Stephen’s BAC at the start of the problem and each of the next four hours.
(b)
Draw a graph showing how Stephen’s BAC changed over time.
(c)
At a BAC of 0.10 it is illegal for Stephen to drive. Approximately when does that happen?
