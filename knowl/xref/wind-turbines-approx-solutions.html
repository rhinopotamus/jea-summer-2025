Exercise 8.

Wind turbines are used to generate electricity. For a particular wind turbine, the equation
\begin{equation*} W = 2.4 S^3 \end{equation*}
can be used to calculate the amount of electricity generated (\(W\) watts) for a given wind speed (\(S\) mph), over a fixed period of time.
Story also appears in 1.1, 1.3, and 3.3 Exercises
(a)
Make a table showing the amount of electricity produced when the wind speed is 10 mph, 25 mph, and 40 mph.
(b)
Draw a graph illustrating this equation.
(c)
Approximate the wind speed that will generate 12,500 watts of electricity.
